Gifs


The Graphics Interchange Format (GIF) is a bitmap image format that was developed by a team at the online services provider CompuServe led by American computer scientist Steve Wilhite and released on 15 June 1987. It has since come into widespread usage on the World Wide Web due to its wide support and portability between applications and operating systems.

The first Gif was made on June 15th, 1987 by Steve Wilhite. It was of a plane flying in the sky.

The Usage of Gifs

Gifs are extremely popular in today's society. They are suitable for sharp-edged line art with a limited number of colors, such as logos. This takes advantage of the format's lossless compression, which favors flat areas of uniform color with well defined edges. They can also be used to store low-color sprite data for games.

    GIFs can be used for small animations and low-resolution video clips, or as reactions in online messaging used to convey emotion and feelings instead of using words. They are popular on social media platforms such as:

  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • Reddit
  • Twitter

History of Gifs

CompuServe introduced GIF on 15 June 1987 to provide a color image format for their file downloading areas. Gif became more popular than this because it was more efficient than the old format in addition to the fact that it was limited to black and white.
