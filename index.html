History of Gifs

The Graphics Interchange Format (GIF) is a bitmap image format that was developed by CompuServe. The team who made the project was led by a computer scientist named Steve Wilhite and was released on 15 June 1987. It has spread all around the world and become extremely popular.

The company made GIF in order to provide a color image format for their file downloading areas. It became more popular than their old encoding format because it was more efficient than the old format in addition to the fact that it was limited to black and white.

Due to CompuServe encouraging more and more companies to adopt the usage of Gif, it was one of the first two image formats commonly used on Websites, the other image format used was black-and-white XBM.

The first Gif was made on June 15th, 1987 by Steve Wilhite. It was of a plane flying in the sky.

The Usage of Gifs

There were two versions of GIF released, the second version released added new features that greatly expanded the usage of GIFs.

Gifs are extremely popular in today's society. Their usage has spread much since its creation, such as logos. Gifs are extremely useful due to the compression that helps maintain the clarity of the gif Gifs can even be used for sprites for games.
